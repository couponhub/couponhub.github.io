Domain Flipping: A Beginner's Guide

Published on January 9, 2026 • 6 min read

Domain flipping is the practice of buying a domain name at a low price and selling it for a profit to someone who needs it for their business or project. In 2026, as the "dot-com" space becomes more crowded, the value of short, memorable, and keyword-rich domains is skyrocketing.

The 3-Step Flipping Process

1

Research and Acquisition

Identify trending niches or industries (like Green Energy, AI Integration, or Personal Finance). Use tools like Namecheap or GoDaddy to find available domains or expired ones with existing traffic.

2

Valuation

Determine what your domain is worth. Look at "comparables"—similar domains that have sold recently. A ".com" is always more valuable than a ".net" or ".org" in the flipping world.

3

Listing and Sale

List your domain on marketplaces like Sedo, Afternic, or Dan.com. Be patient; domain flipping is often about waiting for the right buyer to come to you.
